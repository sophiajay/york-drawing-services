What is Listed Building Consent?

Listed Building Consent is separate from Planning Permission and is often required for works that affect the character, appearance, or historic significance of a listed property.

You may require Listed Building Consent for:

  • Window replacements
  • Door replacements
  • Internal alterations
  • Removal of historic features
  • Structural alterations
  • Extensions to listed buildings
  • Roof alterations
  • New openings in walls
  • Changes to fireplaces
  • Renovation and restoration works
  • Outbuildings and curtilage structures

Many homeowners are surprised to learn that even internal changes can require consent where they affect the building's historic character.

Failure to obtain Listed Building Consent before carrying out work can result in enforcement action, making it important to obtain the correct approvals before work begins.
